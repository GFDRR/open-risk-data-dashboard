METHODOLOGY


The methodology used to organise and contribute data and to score such data is based two main activities:
  • the definition of a key risk dataset
  • the definition of a scoring method for the datasets available for a specific country as compared to the key risk dataset identified

In the fist activity we defined a limited list of key risk datasets together with their definition, their content, including minimum criteria that datasets should meet, as well as a related simple classification scheme for the datasets. All this information is included in the guided contribution form that allows for the classification of the dataset into the Open Data for Resilience Index database.
The identification of key risk datasets is based on a comprehensive review of recent disaster risk studies and is specific to the DRM sector and includes different categories/facets such as type of: hazard (earthquake, water scarcity, volcano, etc.); risk assessment (hazard, exposure, vulnerability); and action (risk identification, reduction, preparedness, financial protection, resilient recovery, etc.)

The datasets are organised in 5 data categories:
  • Base data: Datasets which are required for the overall assessment, such as administrative boundaries, Digital Elevation Model or Aerial Imagery
  • Hazard data: All datasets related to the modelling or, if already modelled, characterisation of the hazard.
  • Exposure data: All datasets describing the exposure at risk.
  • Vulnerability data: All data or models related to the likelihood of suffering damage or loss conditional on a given the intensity of the hazard.
  • Risk data: All quantitative information and indicators related to (economic) risk from previous assignments for validation and verification purposes.

The key risk dataset are defined mainly to the national scale as a scope for analysis. but the dashboard stores and manages also global and local data, specifically defining an applicability level for each dataset:
  • Level 0: Data from global sources, such as HurDat, GAR, GLOFRIS, FLOPROS, WorldPop.
  • Level 1: National Data, from national offices such as meteorological data, land use information, population statistics on postcode level.
  • Level 2: Detailed data, e.g. from higher resolution or more differentiated, such as economic activities or population count on address (building) level.

For more information on the key risk dataset identified you can read the Final report.

The scoring methodology assesses if data are:
  • Within this schema, all key criteria can be identified, specifically if data are:
    • already collected;
    • in digital format;
    • available online;
    • machine-readable;
    • free of charge;
    • open licensed;
    • up to date;
    • and available with its metadata.
  • The scoring will work on three levels:
    • The single dataset
    • The dataset category: Base data, Hazard, Exposure, Vulnerability, Risk
    • The country under analysis
The scoring methodology is a bottom-up approach that aggregates results of the scoring methodology from the dataset scale up to the country scale.
If all criteria to assess openness and accessibility are metthe dataset is scored as 100% open and accessible. If some of the question have negative answer and the criteria are only partially met a score between 0 and 100 will be provided.
The single dataset contributes in turn to the scoring of a data category they belong to: Base data, Hazard, Exposure, Vulnerability and Risk.
The Country scoring is based on the scoring obtained by the data categories: Base data, Hazard, Exposure, Vulnerability and Risk.
Since the ideal solution would be to have a complete data set for each data type for each hazard, this could be penalizing countries that did not invest in knowledge with regards to some specific peril as their incidence in the risk profile of the country is low. To avoid this a threshold based on the information that can be found in Thinkhazard.org is set. If the specific peril is rated low in terms of hazard by Thinkhazard.org this hazard is not considered in the ideal set to be met for the country.
